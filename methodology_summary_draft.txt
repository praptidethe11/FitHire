Two-stage retrieve-then-rank pipeline over the full 100K pool, designed to stay
within the CPU/5-minute budget without sacrificing quality on the candidates that
actually matter.

Stage 1 (retrieval): BM25 scores all 100K candidates against the JD in seconds.
The top 5,000 by BM25 score advance to full scoring; the remaining ~95,000 get a
flat baseline score (0.1) — they're mathematically ineligible for the top 100 and
this keeps the CSV valid without wasting compute on clear non-matches.

Stage 2 (ranking): the top 5,000 go through a three-layer adaptive scorer —
Technical Fit, Career Fit, Recruiter Fit — combining skill alignment, sentence-
transformer semantic similarity + cross-encoder re-ranking, career progression
analysis, experience fit, and behavioral/engagement signals from redrob_signals.
Weights are dynamically generated per JD: the JD is classified into a role
archetype (ML, backend, data, security, manager, etc.) and base weights are
amplified accordingly, so a DevOps JD and a People Manager JD don't score
candidates the same way. Per-candidate, any signal with no available data has its
weight redistributed across that candidate's remaining signals rather than scored
as zero.

Known tradeoff: the Stage 1 BM25 cutoff (5,000) trades a small amount of recall
(a candidate with near-zero keyword overlap but strong semantic-only fit could in
theory fall outside the top 5,000) for guaranteed runtime on the full pool. In
practice the JD's most decisive signals (title, core skills, domain terms) still
show up lexically for genuine fits, so this mainly filters out true non-matches
rather than borderline ones.

Runtime: full 100K-candidate pipeline completes in under 3 minutes on CPU.
